Where Solar Sales Teams
Lose Calling Opportunities

Slow lead response

Interest drops when a new or approved lead waits too long for the first conversation.

Uncalled lead lists

Existing and purchased lists remain untouched when representatives focus only on active deals.

Inconsistent scripts

Representatives ask different questions and produce qualification data that is hard to compare.

Too much sales labor

Salespeople spend valuable time reaching voicemail, retrying numbers, and recording routine outcomes.

Weak follow-up

Interested prospects disappear when callbacks and next actions are not scheduled consistently.

Poor CRM data

Objections, qualification answers, opt-outs, and next steps remain incomplete or unrecorded.

Questions
and Answers

What do we need to provide?

An approved lead source, target market, solar offer, qualification rules, calling policy, calendar, and preferred CRM or handoff process.

Does the AI sound like a real person?

The agent is designed for natural turn-taking and clear speech, but it is not presented as a specific real person and the required campaign disclosures are configured before launch.

Does the caller identify itself as an AI?

Disclosure language is configured according to the campaign, jurisdiction, client policy, and applicable requirements. We do not build the product around deceptive impersonation.

Which solar qualification questions can it ask?

Typical fields include ownership, electricity bill range, interest, property or site context, timeline, financing preference, current objections, and preferred next step.

Can it answer objections and solar questions?

Yes, within an approved knowledge base and response boundary. Pricing promises, technical guarantees, legal claims, and unsupported savings claims can be restricted or routed to a person.

Can it transfer interested prospects to our sales team?

Yes. Qualified calls can be transferred live when a representative is available or converted into a callback task with the full conversation summary.

Can it book directly into our calendars?

Yes. The system can use approved calendars, availability rules, meeting types, territories, and representative routing.

Which CRMs and calling tools can it connect to?

Integration depends on the APIs and access available. Common setups can include CRM records, calendars, webhooks, calling providers, reporting tools, and internal sales systems.

How are opt-outs, consent, recordings, and suppression lists handled?

They are treated as campaign controls, not afterthoughts. The exact setup depends on the lead source, call type, jurisdiction, company policy, and legal review. The client remains responsible for approving the lawful campaign basis.

Can we start with a small campaign?

Yes. A pilot can focus on one approved lead source, one territory, one call flow, and one appointment or transfer outcome.
