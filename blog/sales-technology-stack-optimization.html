üö´ Common Stack Optimization Pitfalls

Tool Bloat: Adding too many tools without clear purpose or proper integration. Every new tool should solve a specific problem and integrate well with existing systems.

Poor Change Management: Implementing new technology without adequate training or change management processes. Success requires user adoption, not just tool deployment.

Integration Neglect: Focusing on individual tool capabilities while ignoring how they work together. The stack's value comes from seamless integration, not isolated functionality.

Insufficient Training: Providing inadequate training and support for new tools. Even the best technology fails without proper user education and ongoing support.
