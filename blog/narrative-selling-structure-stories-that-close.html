Stories organize attention. A good sales narrative reframes the problem, raises the stakes, showcases a turning point, and lands on a credible resolution‚Äîwith proof. Use this structure across decks, demos, and proposals to make your value obvious and memorable.

üéØ Story Beats

  • Status Quo: Reveal costly friction your prospect recognizes.
  • Stakes: Tie delays to revenue, compliance, or reputation.
  • Turning Point: A single mechanism that makes the difference.
  • Resolution: Before/after contrast with concrete metrics.
  • Proof: Matched case fragment; one chart; one quote.

üß± Deck Structure

  1. Open with the problem scene (1 slide, visual).
  2. Quantify stakes (TCO of delay).
  3. Introduce your mechanism (how it works in 90 seconds).
  4. Show a proof snapshot (metric + short quote).
  5. Outline the pilot (milestones, owners, exit criteria).

  • Set the scene: a realistic persona and task.

  • Call out risk‚Äëreduction features (audit logs, permissions).
  • End with a next step inside the product (template, checklist).

üß∞ Proposal Flow

Lead with outcomes and milestones, then supporting features. Add a terms map and appendixes for procurement and IT. Keep the core narrative tight; let stakeholders dive into details as needed.
