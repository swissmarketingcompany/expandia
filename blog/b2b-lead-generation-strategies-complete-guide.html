üéØ 1. Lead Generation Foundations

Successful B2B lead generation starts with understanding your ideal customer profile (ICP) and buyer journey. Companies that clearly define their ICP see 68% higher account win rates and 36% shorter sales cycles.

üéØ ICP Definition Framework

  • Demographics: Company size, industry, revenue
  • Technographics: Tech stack, tools used
  • Behavioral: Buying patterns, decision process
  • Pain Points: Specific challenges they face
  • Triggers: Events that initiate buying

üìä Lead Qualification Criteria

  • Budget: Financial capacity ($X+)
  • Authority: Decision-making power
  • Need: Clear business pain
  • Timeline: Urgency to solve (3-6 months)
  • Fit: Alignment with ICP

‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most effective B2B lead generation strategies?

The most effective strategies combine outbound and inbound: Cold email (2-3% response), LinkedIn outreach (8-12% connection rate), content marketing, account-based marketing, and referral programs. Multi-channel approaches see 3x higher success rates.

How much should I budget for lead generation?

B2B companies typically allocate 8-12% of revenue to marketing, with 40-60% for lead generation. This translates to $2,700-$6,000 monthly for a $1M ARR company. Start with $5,000/month for meaningful results.

What's the average cost per lead in B2B?

B2B cost per lead varies: Email marketing ($42), Content marketing ($80), Social media ($119), Search engines ($135), Webinars ($196). SaaS averages $135 CPL, enterprise software $400+ CPL.

How long does it take to see results?

Outbound generates results in 2-4 weeks, paid advertising in 1-2 weeks, inbound content in 3-6 months. Most companies see meaningful pipeline impact within 30-60 days of implementation.
