B2B Sales Prospecting FAQ

Master the art of B2B sales prospecting with proven techniques and best practices.

What's the difference between prospecting and lead generation?

While often confused, B2B sales prospecting and lead generation serve different purposes in the sales funnel:

Aspect Lead Generation Sales Prospecting
Purpose Attract interest and identify potential buyers Qualify and engage specific prospects
Approach Inbound/content marketing Outbound/direct outreach
Timeline Long-term (3-12 months) Short-term (days to weeks)
Ownership Marketing team Sales team

How do I build an effective prospect list?

Building a high-quality prospect list requires systematic research and qualification:

Step 1: Define Your ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)

Company size, industry, revenue, technology stack, growth stage, pain points

Step 2: Identify Decision Makers

C-level executives, VPs, directors, managers with budget authority

Step 3: Use Research Tools

LinkedIn Sales Navigator, ZoomInfo, Apollo, industry databases

Step 4: Qualify and Prioritize

Score prospects based on fit, timing, budget, and authority (BANT criteria)

How many touchpoints should I have in my prospecting sequence?

Effective prospecting sequences require multiple touchpoints across different channels:

Optimal Sequence Structure:

  • 8-12 total touchpoints over 4-6 weeks
  • 3-4 emails with value-driven content
  • 2-3 LinkedIn messages with social context
  • 2-3 phone calls at strategic intervals
  • 1-2 video messages for personalization

Timing Guidelines:

  • Day 1: Initial email outreach
  • Day 3: LinkedIn connection request
  • Day 7: Follow-up email with value
  • Day 10: Phone call attempt
  • Day 14: LinkedIn message
  • Day 21: Final email with resources

How do I handle common prospecting objections?

Successful prospecting objection handling requires preparation and empathy:

"We're not interested"

Response: "I understand. Many of our best clients said the same thing initially. Can I ask what specifically makes you feel that way? I might have insights that could be valuable."

"We're happy with our current solution"

Response: "That's great to hear! What do you like most about it? I'd love to understand what's working well and perhaps share how others in your industry are enhancing their results."

"Send me information"

Response: "I'd be happy to send relevant information. To make sure it's valuable for your specific situation, could you tell me about your current challenges with [relevant area]?"
